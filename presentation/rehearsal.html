Rehearsal plan — three passes

1
Pass 1 — Solo, script in hand (each person).
Read your Say blocks aloud, out loud, timed. Goal: sound natural, not read. Mark any sentence you stumble on and simplify it in your own words — the script is a floor, not a cage.
2
Pass 2 — Full run with the live demo, STANDARD variant.
Screen-share on, real clicks, real handoffs. Time each section. This is where you find the demo's rough edges — do it at least twice so the click path is muscle memory.
3
Pass 3 — Interruption drill.
One person plays the evaluator and interrupts mid-slide with the questions in §Backup mapping. Practice the "IF INTERRUPTED" line, answer in one sentence, and return to the script. This is what separates a smooth team from a flustered one.
Do one final CORE run the morning of, in case your slot gets cut short — you should be able to drop to 14 minutes without thinking.

Slides teams typically overrun — watch these:
  • Slide 6 (ERD): easy to over-explain. Say the embed-vs-reference rule, the snapshot point, and one index — then move. Deep model talk belongs in backup B5–B8.
  • Slide 10 (Haya demo): the temptation is to click everything. Follow the 8-step path and resist side-trips. If a filter is slow, narrate, don't wait.
  • Slide 11 (auth demo): the 401→refresh step is timing-dependent — do NOT wait for it live. One attempt, then screenshot B12 and keep talking.
  • Slide 12 (checkout): mock payment can lag. If it's not instant, cut to the fulfilment queue with a seeded paid order.
  • Slide 16 (learned): three people, one sentence each — rehearse the handoff so it's crisp, not three mini-speeches.
  • Slide 21 (future): the Lebanon argument is the emotional close — don't rush it, but don't let it sprawl past 90 seconds.
Pacing cue: if you hit the demo handoff (slide 9) and more than 7 minutes are gone, you're slow — tighten the demo to headline actions. If you're at slide 9 under 5 minutes, you can afford the STANDARD depth.

Equipment & environment checklist

  • Laptop charged + charger plugged in (a dying battery mid-demo is the classic failure).
  • Stable internet for the call itself (the app is offline, but your screen-share isn't).
  • Second screen or phone with the presentation_script.html open for the current speaker (don't read from the shared screen).
  • Both servers started and smoke-tested 10 minutes before (see live_demo_script §Pre-demo).
  • Database seeded; one order in delivered state for the customer (review step depends on it).
  • Logged out; DevTools open on Network; browser zoom ~110%.
  • Coupons SAVE10 / FLAT50 confirmed active.
  • Screenshots folder (A1–C26) open and named, for instant fallback.
  • Both .pptx decks open: OptiCart_Main primary, OptiCart_Backup minimised and ready.
  • Notifications silenced (OS + browser); phone on silent.
  • Roles assigned for the mouse: Haya drives Part A & the admin UI on 14; Malik drives Part B; Mahmod drives Part C.
  • Agreed hand-signal for "wrap it up" so a slow speaker gets a nudge without an awkward pause.

Day-of run sheet

  • T-15 min: servers up, seeded, smoke-tested; screenshots open; both decks open.
  • T-5 min: logged out, DevTools/Network open, zoom set, notifications off.
  • T-2 min: decide the variant (CORE/STANDARD/EXTENDED) based on your actual slot; set the toggle in the script.
  • T-0: Malik opens (slide 1). Watch the pacing cue at slide 9.
  • On any deep-dive question: the owner takes it, jumps to the mapped backup slide, answers, returns to Main.
  • On any live failure: switch to the matching screenshot, narrate, recover off-screen — never debug on camera.
  • Close on slide 22; keep the backup deck up during Q&A.
