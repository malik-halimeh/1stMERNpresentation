One-line posture summary

The auth core (rotation, reuse detection, in-memory tokens, RBAC, append-only audit) is genuinely above student-project standard, and the July audit's serious findings — all clustered at third-party trust boundaries (the Stripe webhook, coupon revalidation, env-secret fallbacks) — were fixed and are provable live with test_fixes.ts. What remains open is either a conscious UX tradeoff (register enumeration), an inherent JWT property (role lag), or a scale-out concern (single-instance stores) — and the team can say exactly why for each. The lesson worth presenting: the code you write is usually safer than the seams where systems meet.
